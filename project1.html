Executive Summary

The United States Medicare program reported over $50 billion in annual improper payments across Medicare Fee-for-Service, Part C, and Part D programs in fiscal year 2024. This research presents a sophisticated temporal machine learning framework that addresses this critical national challenge by identifying high-risk providers warranting investigation before improper payments occur, fundamentally shifting the paradigm from reactive recovery to proactive prevention.

Key Achievement: The system processes over 26 gigabytes of federal Medicare data covering 1.38 million healthcare providers to identify patterns indicative of compliance risks and anomalous prescribing behavior, achieving 72.46% accuracy in temporal validation while identifying 26 OIG exclusion cases annually within the top 1,000 risk-scored providers.

Through rigorous temporal validation—training exclusively on historical data and testing on future periods—this framework demonstrates measurable financial impact with projected savings of $2.6 million annually from investigating just 0.07% of providers. The system's precision at the highest risk tier reaches 3.3%, representing a 33-fold improvement over random selection.

Methodology

Data Integration Framework

This research integrates five authoritative federal datasets spanning 2022-2023:

  • Medicare Part D Prescribers: 1.38 million providers with 84 variables including claims, costs, and beneficiary demographics
  • Open Payments Database: 919,579 providers with approximately $12.6 billion in annual industry payments
  • LEIE Exclusions: 81,914 excluded individuals/entities for exclusion validation
  • Medicare Part D by Drug: Approximately 26 million provider-drug combinations
  • Medicare Part D by Geography: 115,396 geographic-drug combinations for peer benchmarking

Machine Learning Approach

XGBoost Random Forest SHAP Explainability Temporal Validation Ensemble Methods

Three complementary algorithms are implemented: Logistic Regression for baseline establishment (AUC: 0.613), Random Forest for non-linear pattern detection (AUC: 0.705), and XGBoost for gradient boosting optimization (AUC: 0.724). The final ensemble model combines predictions using weighted voting with a 2:1 XGBoost to Random Forest ratio.

Policy Implications

This work directly advances federal priorities established in:

  • Medicare Access and CHIP Reauthorization Act (MACRA) of 2015: Supports program integrity provisions by identifying high-risk providers before payments occur
  • 21st Century Cures Act: Advances interoperability mandates through successful integration of five distinct federal databases
  • Executive Order 13985 on Advancing Equity: Ensures equitable access to Medicare services while identifying compliance risks
  • CMS Program Integrity Strategic Plan 2022-2026: Aligns with goals for predictive analytics deployment

National Impact: The broader implications extend beyond Medicare to the entire U.S. healthcare system. The framework's methodology can be adapted for Medicaid, commercial insurance, and Veterans Affairs programs, potentially preventing billions in improper payments across all healthcare sectors.

Technical Implementation

Core Algorithm Parameters

  • XGBoost: n_estimators=200, max_depth=6, learning_rate=0.05, scale_pos_weight=50
  • Random Forest: n_estimators=300, max_depth=10, min_samples_split=5
  • Ensemble Weighting: 66.7% XGBoost, 33.3% Random Forest
  • Temporal Validation: Train on 2022 data, test on 2023 data

Key Risk Indicators (SHAP Importance)

  • Total Claims Volume: 9.8% importance
  • Opioid Prescription Share: 9.6% importance
  • Beneficiary Risk Score: 8.6% importance
  • Cost per Claim Ratio: 8.0% importance
  • Brand Drug Preference: 7.8% importance
