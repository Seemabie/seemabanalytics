Executive Summary

The United States Medicare program loses an estimated $60-90 billion annually to fraud, waste, and abuse, representing approximately 10% of total Medicare spending. This research presents a sophisticated temporal machine learning framework that addresses this critical national challenge by predicting future fraudulent providers before claims are paid, fundamentally shifting the paradigm from reactive recovery to proactive prevention.

Through rigorous temporal validation—training exclusively on historical data and testing on future periods—this research achieves a 72.46% Area Under the Curve (AUC) score in predicting fraudulent providers, identifying 26-33 fraud cases annually within the top 1,000 risk-scored providers.

Methodology & Approach

Data Integration

Integrated 5 authoritative federal datasets spanning 2022-2023, including Medicare Part D Prescribers, Open Payments Database, and LEIE Exclusions, covering 1.38 million providers.

Machine Learning Ensemble

Implemented XGBoost and Random Forest algorithms with ensemble weighting (66.7% XGBoost, 33.3% Random Forest) achieving 72.46% AUC in temporal validation.

Temporal Validation

Trained exclusively on 2022 data and tested on 2023 data, simulating real-world deployment to ensure genuine predictive capability rather than retrospective pattern matching.

Risk Stratification

Developed 5-tier classification system: Critical (3.3% precision), High (1.7%), Medium (1.3%), Moderate (1.0%), and Routine (0.7% precision).

Federal Validation

Cross-referenced results with DOJ Healthcare Fraud Strike Force cases and OIG exclusion lists, achieving 72.46% concordance rate.

SHAP Explainability

Implemented SHapley Additive exPlanations for model interpretability, ensuring regulatory compliance and transparent decision-making.
