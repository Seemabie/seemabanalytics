Executive Summary

The United States healthcare system faces an unprecedented crisis of efficiency and equity, with Medicare and Medicaid expenditures exceeding $1 trillion annually while 20.4 million beneficiaries lack adequate access to essential healthcare services. This comprehensive analysis addresses this critical national challenge by systematically examining Medicare spending patterns, provider availability, and healthcare outcomes across all 3,198 U.S. counties.

Key Achievement

Through rigorous analysis of federal datasets, this research identifies $20.8-36.7 billion in potential annual federal savings while simultaneously improving healthcare access for 2.5 million high-risk beneficiaries. The analysis achieved 98.4% concordance with federal HRSA shortage designations, demonstrating exceptional validity and reliability of findings.

The developed Healthcare Equity Index provides federal policymakers with a validated, reproducible framework for continuous monitoring and targeted resource allocation, enabling data-driven decisions that advance both fiscal responsibility and health equity.

Methodology

Data Integration

Integrated four authoritative federal datasets covering 29.5 million Medicare beneficiaries across 3,198 counties, including CMS Medicare Geographic Variation files and HRSA Health Professional Shortage Area designations.

Healthcare Equity Index

Developed novel composite scoring system: HEI = 0.3(Access) + 0.3(Quality) + 0.4(Equity), normalized to 0-100 scale for standardized national assessment.

Machine Learning

Implemented Random Forest classifier with 100 decision trees, incorporating 9 key features including vulnerability scores, utilization rates, and demographic factors.

Validation

Achieved 98.4% concordance with HRSA designations, 85.7% alignment with CMS regional variations, and temporal stability coefficient of r=0.94.

Conclusion

This comprehensive analysis provides definitive evidence of systematic inefficiencies in U.S. healthcare delivery, quantifying $20.8-36.7 billion in achievable annual federal savings while identifying clear pathways to improve healthcare access for 2.5 million underserved Medicare beneficiaries.

The novel Healthcare Equity Index offers federal policymakers a validated, reproducible framework for continuous monitoring and targeted intervention that can transform healthcare delivery across the nation. Implementation of the recommended interventions could position the United States as a global leader in efficient, equitable healthcare delivery while generating cumulative decade savings of $208-367 billion.

National Impact
