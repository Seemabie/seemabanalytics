Executive Summary

The Medicare Telehealth Program Integrity System addresses a documented gap in oversight following the 88 fold expansion of telehealth services during the COVID 19 pandemic. More than 28 million beneficiaries used telehealth during the first year of the pandemic, creating program integrity vulnerabilities. The HHS Office of Inspector General identified 1,714 providers whose telehealth billing poses high risk to Medicare. This system developed a Combined Risk Score framework integrating statistical z score analysis with Isolation Forest machine learning to identify anomalous billing patterns.

Key Finding: Analysis of 50,000 Medicare telehealth providers covering 322.4 million services (2019 to 2023) identified 35 providers (0.07%) with billing patterns between 200% and 30,500% above peer benchmarks. These critical risk providers collectively accounted for $420.2 million in excess payments above peer median rates.

The methodology achieved 91.3% accuracy with 100% precision in critical risk identification through dual validation across six federal databases. The framework generates provider risk scores from 0 to 100 by systematically comparing billing behavior against specialty and geographic peer benchmarks.

Methodology

Statistical Analysis (15% Weight)

Z score analysis for univariate outlier detection, calculating provider specific deviations from specialty and geographic peer groups. Providers exceeding 3 standard deviations above peer benchmarks trigger initial anomaly flags. Multivariate analysis using Mahalanobis distance identifies unusual combinations of billing patterns.

Machine Learning (85% Weight)

Isolation Forest algorithm processes 5 key features: service volume anomaly (38% importance), payment variance (27%), geographic risk factor (18%), temporal growth pattern (11%), and service diversity index (6%). Contamination parameter of 0.01 targets top 1% unusual patterns.

Combined Risk Score

Integrates statistical z scores (15% weight) and ML anomaly scores (85% weight) into scores from 0 to 100. Geographic risk adjustment adds 10 points for high risk states. Temporal flags trigger for greater than 200% year over year billing increases.

Validation Framework

10 fold cross validation confirmed 91.3% accuracy (± 2.4%). Bootstrap resampling (1,000 iterations) produced stable 95% confidence intervals. Sensitivity analysis demonstrated results robust to ±20% parameter variations.

Conclusion

This research developed and validated a Combined Risk Score framework for Medicare telehealth program integrity, identifying 35 providers (0.07%) with billing patterns between 200% and 30,500% above peer benchmarks, collectively accounting for $420.2 million in excess payments. The methodology achieved 91.3% accuracy with 100% precision in critical risk identification through integrated statistical and machine learning analysis across six federal databases.

The geographic concentration of findings in DOJ Strike Force priority regions (Florida, California, Texas) and alignment with OIG identified high risk patterns provides independent validation of the detection methodology. The system demonstrates scalability for systematic analysis across the Medicare provider population.

Important Note: This system identifies statistical anomalies in billing patterns warranting further investigation. Determination of actual improper payments requires investigation by authorized agencies. The findings represent statistical deviations from peer benchmarks, not confirmed fraud or abuse.
